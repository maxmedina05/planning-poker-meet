Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

Planning Poker is a Google Meet add-on that helps teams estimate story points during meetings. This policy explains what data is collected and how it is handled.

What we collect

  • Anonymous session ID: When you open the add-on, Firebase Anonymous Authentication assigns you a randomly generated unique ID (e.g. abc123xyz). This ID contains no personal information — it is not linked to your name, email address, or Google account.
  • Votes: The card value you submit during a session (e.g. "5", "8", "?") is stored in Google Cloud Firestore under your anonymous ID for the duration of the meeting.
  • Story title: If the facilitator enters a story title, it is stored in Firestore for the duration of the session.

What we do NOT collect

  • Your name, email address, or any Google account information
  • Your Google Meet meeting content, chat, or video
  • Any data beyond what is listed above

How data is stored and deleted

All session data is stored in Google Cloud Firestore (Google's infrastructure). Each room document is automatically deleted 24 hours after the last activity in that session via a Firestore TTL (Time-to-Live) policy. No manual deletion is required.

Data sharing

We do not sell, share, or transfer any data to third parties. Data is stored solely on Google Cloud infrastructure and is only accessible to participants in the same meeting session.

Third-party services

  • Google Firebase — Authentication and database. See Firebase Privacy.
  • Google reCAPTCHA v3 — Bot protection (App Check). See Google Privacy Policy.
